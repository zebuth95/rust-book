# Unrecoverable errors with panic
Sometimes bad things happen in your code, and there’s nothing you can do about it. In these cases, Rust has the panic! macro.

When a **panic!** occurs, the program will either **unwind** the stack,
cleaning up data as it goes, or **abort** the process immediately.
The latter is more efficient but bypasses cleanup. **panic!** is used for bugs,
failed assertions, and situations where you have no logical way to proceed.
